AI Cost Forecasting: Budgeting for 2026-2027

AI API prices have dropped 50-80% per year since 2023. But your usage will likely grow faster than prices fall. Here's how to build a realistic 12-month AI budget — with formulas, templates, and real-world scenarios.

The Core Paradox

"Prices drop 50%/year — so my bill should halve, right?" Wrong. Most teams increase AI usage by 3-10x/year as they launch new features. The net effect: your bill still grows, just slower than it would have.

The Forecasting Formula

Next Year Budget = Current Monthly Cost × Usage Growth Multiplier × (1 - Price Drop Rate) × 1.5  (safety padding)

Worked Example: You spend $1,000/mo today. You're at Seed stage (3x growth). Price drop ~25% next 12 months. Padding = 1.5x.

$1,000 × 3.0 × (1 - 0.25) × 1.5 = $1,000 × 3.0 × 0.75 × 1.5 = $3,375/month (12 months from now) = $28,875 total next-year budget

For Chinese Developers & Companies

The AI cost landscape in China has unique characteristics that affect forecasting:

  • Proxy costs: Accessing GPT-4o/Claude via proxy adds 20-40% markup. Factor this in — or switch to domestic models.
  • Domestic model quality: Qwen3 and DeepSeek V3 are now competitive with GPT-4o on Chinese-language tasks. The cost gap justifies switching.
  • Regulatory: Some provinces offer GPU/compute subsidies for AI companies. These can reduce self-hosting costs by 30-50%.
  • Forecast template (China): Separate domestic model costs (RMB) from international model costs (USD). The exchange rate matters.

Actionable Takeaways

  1. Build your forecast today: Use the formula. Even a rough forecast beats no forecast.
  2. Review quarterly: AI pricing changes too fast for annual reviews.
  3. Pad by 50%: Unexpected model upgrades, new features, and traffic spikes will happen.
  4. Separate domestic/international (China teams): The optimal model mix changes quarterly.
  5. Use the forecast to negotiate: Providers discount for committed spend. Show them your growth numbers.
